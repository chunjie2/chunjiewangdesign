This single stair housing project in Denver, Colorado,   is rooted in the concept of setbacks, which define the structure’s spatial and functional character. The design uses setbacks to create negative spaces that provide open areas and private front and back yards for each unit. These setbacks also introduce outdoor spaces where tenants can interact with nature. At the core of the building is a single stair core, simplifying circulation and maximizing the efficient use of space.

The building emphasizes the contrast between heavy and light materials. The units are constructed with masonry, creating a grounded and durable structure. In contrast, the facade features a metal mesh structure that incorporates hanging gardens. These gardens reduce the building’s visual mass and serve as functional green spaces, offering shading, natural cooling, and a connection to the outdoors. The mesh structure and metal decking frame the gardens and add openness to the building’s overall design.
Each floor contains two family units with 2-3 bedrooms, offering interiors connected to private outdoor areas.
